About

I am a theoretical physicist and data scientist with a Ph.D. in Astrophysics & Cosmology, developing frameworks to understand cosmic structure formation and fundamental physics. My research focuses on the 21-cm signal and line-intensity mapping as cosmological probes — advancing models connecting these signals to dark matter, neutrinos, and the early Universe. I develop bispectrum-based higher-order statistical methods and ML-driven computational frameworks that translate theoretical predictions into testable observables.

Alongside my research career, I have applied these same mathematical foundations — stochastic modeling, Bayesian inference, high-dimensional parameter estimation — to quantitative finance and applied data science, including derivatives pricing, portfolio optimization, and time-series forecasting. I thrive at the intersection of rigorous mathematics and real-world impact, whether calibrating volatility surfaces or probing the cosmic dawn.

Research Interests
